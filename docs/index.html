Tuned to You

Not a generic setup guide. An engineer that adapts to your driving style, your preferences, and your car.

🎙️

Talk Like You're on the Radio

Hold push-to-talk and describe what you feel. "I keep spinning out on corner exit" or "I want more angle on entry." The AI adapts to your preferences — whether you like a loose rear or a planted, predictable car.

📊

It Watches Your Driving

While you lap, AI Tuner reads tire temps, suspension compression, slip angles, and G-forces from your live telemetry — 10 times per second, per corner. It builds a picture of how you brake, turn in, and get on the power. Your style, quantified.

🧠

Understands Tuning

Powered by Google Gemini AI with a detailed system prompt grounded in racing physics. It knows that rear oversteer + high tire temps often means too much camber or too stiff ARBs. It gives you relative adjustments: "Soften rear springs by 2 clicks."

💾

Remembers Your Builds

Every tune is saved per car, per discipline. Built a drift Silvia last week? Switch to your rally Impreza and back — your engineer remembers what you changed, what worked, and what you like. Your preferences carry over between sessions.

🏎️

Adapts to Your Discipline

Grip racing, drifting (low/mid/high HP), rally, or drag — tell it what you're doing and it adjusts its entire approach. It won't push you toward a grip setup when you want a loose drift car. Your goals, your way.

🪟

Stays Out of Your Way

A tiny transparent overlay that sits on top of your game. Shows speed, RPM, and your engineer's messages. Toggle it with a hotkey — it never interrupts your driving.

Example Conversations

The engineer responds to your specific situation — not canned advice.

💬

You: "The rear keeps stepping out mid-corner, especially in long sweepers."

Engineer: "Your rear tires are 20°C hotter than fronts and I'm seeing 1.4+ slip ratio spikes. Try adding 0.2 Bar rear tire pressure and softening the rear ARB by 2-3 clicks. That'll give you more mechanical grip without killing rotation on entry."

💬

You: "Car feels planted now but it's understeering into tight corners."

Engineer: "Good — the rear is balanced now. For the front, I'd drop front tire pressure by 0.1 Bar and add half a degree of negative camber. Your front suspension is bottoming out 40% of the time, so stiffen front springs by 1 click too."

💬

You: "I want more angle on my drift entries."

Engineer: "For snappier initiation, try adding 1-2 degrees of front toe-out and softening the front rebound by 2 clicks. That'll let the weight transfer faster on flick. Your current slip angles peak at 28° — this should push you past 35°."

Setup in 5 Minutes

1

Turn on Forza Data Out & Borderless Windowed

In FH6 Settings → HUD & Gameplay, enable "Data Out" and set the port to 5300. Also switch your display mode to Borderless Windowed (not Fullscreen) — the overlay can only appear on top of your game in this mode.

2

Launch AI Tuner & Paste Your API Key

Download, run the app, and paste your Google Gemini API key in Settings. That's it — the overlay appears on screen and connects to telemetry automatically.

3

Pick a Car and Drive

Jump in any car. The engineer detects a new vehicle and asks you to confirm the name and what you're doing (racing, drifting, rally, drag). Tap the quick buttons — takes 2 seconds.

4

Let It Learn Your Driving

Drive normally for a few minutes. The AI builds a profile of your car's behavior — which tires overheat, where you lose grip, how hard you're pushing.

5

Ask for Help, Get Faster

Hold push-to-talk and describe what feels off. The engineer combines your feedback with hard telemetry data to give you precise, actionable changes. Go to the garage, apply them, and feel the difference.
